SETUP COMPLETE!

You've assembled the electrolysis apparatus! The battery provides electrical energy, the graphite pencil electrodes dip into the water, and the salt water acts as the electrolyte — carrying the current.

⚡ SCIENCE FACT: Pure water barely conducts electricity.
Salt (NaCl) dissolves into Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions that carry
the electric charge through the solution!
⚡

CIRCUIT COMPLETE!

Electricity is now flowing! The positive (+) wire connects to one electrode and the negative (−) wire to the other. The current passing through the salt water is splitting the water molecules!

🔬 SCIENCE FACT: You should see TWICE as many bubbles
at the negative electrode as at the positive one.
That's because H₂O has 2 hydrogen atoms but only 1 oxygen!
