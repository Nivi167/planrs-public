🔀 nPr vs nCr — Does Order Matter?

Experiment with the values of n and r below. Watch how the numbers change — and discover WHY permutations are always ≥ combinations!

📐 Permutation (Order matters)
ⁿPᵣ = n! / (n−r)!
Arrange r items from n
Different orders = different results
🎲 Combination (Order doesn't matter)
ⁿCᵣ = n! / r!(n−r)!
Choose r items from n
Same group, any order = same result
ⁿPᵣ (Permutations)
60
ⁿCᵣ (Combinations)
10
🧙 Example: Choosing 3 students from 5 for a race:
There are 60 ways to assign 1st, 2nd, 3rd place (order matters — Permutation!)
But only 10 ways to pick a team of 3 (order doesn't matter — Combination!)
