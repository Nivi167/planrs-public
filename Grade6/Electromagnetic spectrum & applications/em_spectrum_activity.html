Why each wavelength is used:
Radio: Long wavelength → passes through walls & atmosphere → perfect for broadcasting & WiFi.
Microwave: Absorbed by water molecules → heats food; also used in radar & satellite comms.
Infrared: All warm objects emit IR → thermal cameras detect body heat; TV remotes use near-IR.
Visible: The narrow band our eyes evolved to detect → photography, traffic lights, fibre optics.
Ultraviolet: High energy → kills bacteria (sterilisation), causes tanning; bees can see UV patterns on flowers.
X-Ray: Very short wavelength → penetrates soft tissue but blocked by bone → medical imaging, airport security.
Gamma: Highest energy → kills cancer cells (radiotherapy); emitted by radioactive decay.
